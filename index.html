The Main Outcomes: States and Policy

COP26 has been crucial in moulding the international focus and inter-state cooperation regarding the climate crisis. But has it been sufficient so far?

International trade and multinational production of certain products are paramount contributors to carbon emissions.

Therefore, any new policies regarding trade regulation are important to look into.

Ecologies and Biodiversity:

The loss of biodiversity and the extinction of several species has been a great concern in the past century. Between 1970 and 2014, the effects of global warming have been inextricably linked to the extinction of 4,000 species.

COP26 has the ability to call for more effective measures against the exctinction of thousands of animals.

Largest Polluters:

The world's wealthiest people make a huge contribution to climate change through carbon-hungry activities. How can we reduce emissions from the rich?
